## Jitrois, an independent French luxury house

Before forming an opinion on **Jitrois**, it helps to know where the House comes from. Jitrois is an independent French house, born in the 1980s and still guided by its founding vision. Its Paris boutique stands at 38 rue du Faubourg Saint-Honoré, in the 8th arrondissement, a few steps from the great couture houses.

### A founder who came from the body, not from fashion

The journey of [Jean-Claude Jitrois](https://jitrois.com/pages/jean-claude-jitrois) sheds light on everything the House creates. Trained in psychomotricity and therapeutic practice, he placed the body, movement and freedom of gesture at the centre of his work long before any aesthetic consideration. For him, the skin is not a surface but an interface, and the garment must reveal the body without ever constraining it.

In the 1980s, he brought leather into the realm of couture. Colour, shoulder volumes, defined waists, embroidery, sculpted dresses, leather became a creative fabric shaped like haute couture. This was the birth of **leather couture**, a story we tell on the [heritage page of the House](https://jitrois.com/pages/our-heritage).

## The invention of stretch leather, the fact behind the reputation

If you were to remember one single fact to assess the brand, it would be this one. In the early 1990s, Jitrois invented **stretch leather**, a decisive innovation in the history of the material. The grain side of plongé lambskin, carefully preserved, is bonded onto a cotton and Lycra base. The leather gains controlled elasticity without losing any of its nobility.

For the first time, leather could follow the body like a true second skin. This invention is publicly documented, the [Jean-Claude Jitrois Wikipedia page](https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean-Claude_Jitrois) credits the designer as the inventor of stretch leather.

### What stretch leather changes in practice

- A fitted cut remains comfortable, the leather follows every movement instead of restricting it.
- Pieces hug the silhouette without any armour effect, allowing constructions impossible with classic leather.
- The material keeps the qualities of plongé lambskin, softness, touch and durability over time.

## A recognition that goes beyond the garment

The reputation of the House does not rest on its own discourse alone. It was built alongside artists, musicians and performers, for whom the stage is a space of physical as much as psychological intensity. Jitrois creations accompany them in these moments of extreme exposure, offering structure, protection and freedom of movement.

> "Jitrois is recognised as the French House that reinvented leather, and continues to reinvent it, in colour, in form, in technique, and in its intimate relationship with the body."

This singular position, that of a house identified with a material it transformed itself, is rare in the luxury landscape. Few houses can claim a documented technical invention that carries their name.
