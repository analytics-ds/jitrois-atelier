Wearing leather from head to toe once felt intimidating. Yet it is one of the strongest looks in a woman's wardrobe, provided you master its grammar. A successful **women's all-leather look** does not rest on the number of pieces, but on their coherence. Here is how we, at Jitrois, think about this silhouette, entirely worked in the material that is our signature.

## What makes a successful all-leather silhouette

An outfit worn entirely in leather is judged on its unity. Three principles carry it.

The first is material consistency. Mixing a glossy smooth leather with a matte grain or an imitation breaks the harmony the eye is looking for. A wardrobe from a single hand, in a single material, guarantees the continuity the look demands.

The second is tone. A strong silhouette plays on one deep colour or a range of neighbouring shades. You can vary textures, a supple leather with a velvety suede, to add depth without ever breaking the whole.

The third is balance of volumes. One piece dominates, a structured jacket or wide trousers, while the rest recedes. It is this hierarchy that sets a controlled look apart from an excess of material.

## Start with two key pieces

There is no need to wear everything in leather from day one. The House recommends starting from two pieces that echo each other. A jacket and trousers in stretch leather, in a shared tone, make an elegant, easy-to-live-in base. You then complete the look, at your own pace, with a top, a skirt or a shirt.

The leather dress offers a second, more immediate route. It forms a complete look on its own, without risk of a false note, whether accessorised with understated pieces or left to speak alone. To refine the choice of the lead piece, our guide to [wearing a leather jacket for women](/en/blog/how-to-wear-a-leather-jacket-women/) and to [wearing a leather skirt](/en/blog/how-to-wear-a-leather-skirt/) detail each register. [Leather trousers](/en/blog/how-to-wear-leather-trousers/) remain the safe bet of a structured silhouette.

## Play with materials and textures

Leather is not a uniform material. Between a supple plunged lambskin, a velvety stretch suede and a smooth leather that catches the light, the nuances are endless. Pairing two close finishes, without opposing them, enriches the silhouette and gives it depth.

To breathe, an all-leather outfit benefits from a softer material alongside it, a fine knit under a jacket, a crew-neck sweater slipped under a jumpsuit. This contrast does not weaken the statement, it makes it wearable every day.

## The tones that flatter leather

Black remains the obvious choice, timeless and graphic. But colour has its place in a leather silhouette. Deep tones, tobacco, burnt blood, dark green, extend the sophistication of black while asserting a personality. A range of neighbouring shades keeps the whole united, where bold, opposing colours would weaken it.

## The House's point of view

At Jitrois, the all-leather silhouette is not a challenge but a given, because we dress the whole body in a single material, the stretch leather we invented. Jacket, trousers, dress, top and skirt are born of the same craftsmanship, in our atelier, and are conceived as a wardrobe that recombines.

This is the whole difference of a leather couture look. Stretch leather hugs the silhouette like a second skin, it follows movement, it gives the whole a fluid line that stiff leather does not allow. Composing an outfit entirely in leather then becomes a natural, almost obvious gesture. This material is the heart of what we call [leather couture](/en/blog/what-is-leather-couture/), and you can discover its origin on the Maison's page dedicated to [stretch leather](https://jitrois.com/pages/materials).
