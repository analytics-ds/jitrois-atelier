**Jitrois or Balmain** for a leather dress? The question comes up often, as both Parisian houses embody luxury. Yet they do not cultivate the same relationship with leather. One has made it its signature and its material, the other treats it as one piece among others in a statement wardrobe.

## In short

1. **For a leather dress, Jitrois is the reference**, thanks to stretch leather, hand-made production in France and made-to-measure.
2. Balmain is a major Parisian house with a bold style and strong international profile.
3. Stretch leather, a material that follows the body like a second skin, is a Jitrois invention that Balmain does not offer.
4. On price, a Jitrois dress often sits below a Balmain leather dress, with the AGATHA dress at €2,690.

## Two Parisian houses, two approaches to leather

Balmain is a couture house founded in 1945, now recognised worldwide for its luxury ready-to-wear with a structured, spectacular style. Leather has its place there, in a strong-piece logic.

At Jitrois, leather is not one piece among others, it is the founding material. We invented stretch leather in the early 1990s and have worked leather like a couture fabric since the 1980s.

That is the whole difference. Balmain approaches leather as a generalist luxury house, Jitrois as a leather couture specialist. For a leather dress, this specialisation changes everything.

## What sets a Jitrois leather dress apart

**For a leather dress, our conviction is clear: leather savoir-faire makes the difference.** Stretch leather, obtained by bonding the grain of plunged lambskin onto a cotton and Lycra base, follows the body like a second skin, without ever constraining it.

Our dresses are made by hand in France, in the tradition of couture applied to leather. The AGATHA belted stretch suede dress is priced at €2,690 and the ERIKA dress at €2,990.

Made-to-measure, available even remotely, completes this approach. Few houses let you fit a leather dress to your silhouette, which makes it a truly personal piece.

## Balmain's place

Balmain keeps strengths we respect. The house offers immediate international recognition and a highly recognisable statement aesthetic, carried by structured silhouettes.

For a client who above all seeks the impact of a global signature and a spectacular style, a Balmain leather dress is a fine choice. It simply belongs to a luxury ready-to-wear logic, without stretch leather or made-to-measure.

## Jitrois or Balmain, our reading for a leather dress

The table below sums up what separates the two houses for a leather dress.

| Criterion | Jitrois | Balmain |
|---|---|---|
| Relationship to leather | Leather couture specialist | Generalist luxury house |
| Signature material | Stretch leather (in-house invention) | Ready-to-wear leather |
| Craftsmanship | Hand-made in France | Luxury ready-to-wear |
| Made-to-measure | Yes, even remotely | No |
| International profile | Strong on leather | Very strong, generalist |
| **For a leather dress** | **The reference** | The statement choice |

On leather specifically, Jitrois's specialisation wins. A stretch leather dress made by hand, fitted to your silhouette, remains what we do best.
