**Stretch leather** has transformed the way leather is worn. Long reserved for structured, rigid pieces, leather became, thanks to this innovation, a supple material able to fit the body. This page explains what stretch leather is, how it is made and what sets it apart from traditional leather.

## In brief

1. Stretch leather is genuine leather made extensible, which follows the movements of the body.
2. It combines a very fine hide, often lambskin, with an elastic material on the reverse.
3. Its second-skin nickname comes from its ability to hug the silhouette without constraining it.
4. Maison Jitrois is at its origin and brought it into the vocabulary of couture.
5. It differs from classic leather through its elasticity and the finesse of its cuts.

## A simple definition of stretch leather

Stretch leather is an **extensible leather** obtained by bonding a fine hide to an elastic textile backing. The visible face remains a full-grain leather, with its characteristic grain and touch. The reverse incorporates a supple weave that allows stretching in one or two directions.

The result is a material that behaves like a fabric while keeping the nobility of leather. A stretch-leather piece follows the curve of the back, the line of a leg or the shape of a shoulder, then returns to form. It is this property that brought leather into the realm of **couture**.

## How stretch leather is made

Its making rests on a delicate balance between finesse and strength. The chosen hide, usually a **plonge lambskin**, is selected for its suppleness and regularity. It is then assembled with an elastic base, without the adhesive stiffening the whole.

This work calls for precise **craftsmanship**, where the quality of the hide and the mastery of the assembly determine the final result. The subject is detailed in the article on the [making of leather couture]({{< relref lang="en" path="blog/leather-couture-craftsmanship.md" >}}), which shows how a piece moves from raw hide to finished garment.

## Stretch leather or classic leather: the differences

| Criterion | Classic leather | Stretch leather |
|---|---|---|
| Elasticity | None, fixed shape | Extensible, returns to form |
| Cut | Structured, loose | Close to the body, fitted |
| Freedom of movement | Limited | Free, follows the body |
| Hide thickness | Variable, often thick | Very fine |
| Look | Rigid, architectural | Supple, fluid |

The main difference therefore lies in **movement**. Classic leather trousers impose their structure; a stretch-leather piece accompanies the body. This freedom explains why stretch leather established itself on fitted garments such as [leather trousers]({{< relref lang="en" path="blog/how-to-wear-leather-trousers.md" >}}), the dress or the jumpsuit.

## Second skin, the signature of a house

The image of the **second skin** is not just a phrase. It sums up a vision of leather as a living material, worn as close to the body as possible without ever restraining it. This idea was born at Jitrois, the Parisian house that invented stretch leather and made it its signature, as told in the portrait of [Jean-Claude Jitrois]({{< relref lang="en" path="blog/jean-claude-jitrois-stretch-leather.md" >}}).

Today, stretch leather embodies a certain idea of luxury: that of an exceptional garment that sacrifices nothing to comfort. The collection pieces can be discovered at [jitrois.com](https://jitrois.com/).
