The **leather jacket** is probably the most universal piece in the female wardrobe. It crosses generations, styles and seasons without ever going out of fashion, provided you choose the right model and know how to accompany it. At Jitrois, the jacket is an exercise in construction that we have practised since the 1980s. Here is how to wear it, model by model and occasion by occasion.

## In Brief

1. Choose the model according to your wardrobe, the biker for character, the fitted jacket for versatility, the leather blazer for a tailored allure.
2. In the daytime, the equation of raw denim, a quality t-shirt and a leather jacket remains unbeatable.
3. Over a dress, the jacket deepens and modernises, it is the most feminine pairing.
4. A coloured jacket calls for a neutral outfit, a colourful outfit calls for a black jacket.
5. Comfort depends on the material, a supple leather jacket can be worn for hours, shoulders free.

## Choosing Your Model

Three main families structure the offer, and the choice depends less on trends than on your existing wardrobe.

The **biker jacket** first, with its lapel collar and asymmetric zip. It is the character jacket par excellence, the one that instantly modernises a floral dress or a lace skirt.

The **fitted jacket** next, refined, close to the body, without the codes of the blouson. It is the most versatile, moving from denim to tailored trousers without changing register. The material must follow the body, however, which is the whole point of the [stretch leather](https://jitrois.com/pages/materials) we work, a fitted jacket that constrains neither the shoulders nor the gestures.

The **leather blazer** finally, longer, more constructed, borrowing from the tailoring wardrobe. It is the most dressed choice, remarkable over fluid trousers.

## Daytime, the Art of Simple Contrast

The classic equation remains the best starting point, raw or straight denim, a quality white t-shirt or a fluid blouse, the leather jacket on top. The silhouette is clean, seemingly effortless.

The underlying principle is the one we apply to every piece of the House, the contrast of materials. Leather is dense, it balances with cotton, fine knits, denim, washed silk. It is the same logic that governs how to [wear a leather skirt](/en/blog/how-to-wear-a-leather-skirt/) or how to [wear leather trousers](/en/blog/how-to-wear-leather-trousers/), never two strong shiny pieces on top of each other, always a breath between materials.

## Evening, the Dress as an Ally

Over a dress, the leather jacket produces one of the most beautiful contrasts in the female wardrobe. A fluid or floral dress gains immediate modernity, a little black dress gains character. Wear the jacket open, shoulders relaxed, to preserve the line of the dress, and step onto fine heels to stretch the silhouette.

It is in this exercise that the cut makes all the difference. A jacket built on the principles of [leather couture](/en/blog/what-is-leather-couture/), with drawn shoulders and a followed waist, holds an evening allure effortlessly, where a generic blouson falls back into the casual register.

## Colour, One Simple Rule

Black goes with everything, that much is obvious. Brown and cognac warm up daytime outfits and flatter neutral tones. For a coloured jacket, burgundy, deep green or red, simply reverse the logic, the rest of the outfit stays discreet and lets the piece speak.

One rule crosses all cases, one strong piece at a time. It is the very principle of the couture wardrobe, each piece is designed to structure a silhouette, not to compete with another, a state of mind we explore in our article on the [difference between haute couture and ready-to-wear](/en/blog/haute-couture-vs-ready-to-wear/).

## A Piece for Almost Every Season

Spring and autumn are its natural seasons, the material cuts the wind and keeps you warm without bulk. In winter, the jacket layers over a fine roll neck, in summer, it dresses cool evenings. Few pieces offer such a range of use, which is what makes it a wardrobe investment rather than a seasonal purchase.
