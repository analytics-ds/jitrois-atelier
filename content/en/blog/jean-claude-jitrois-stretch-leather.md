**Jean-Claude Jitrois** holds a singular place in French fashion: that of the designer who brought leather into the vocabulary of couture. By inventing stretch leather, he turned a material reputed to be rigid into a supple, fitted second skin. This portrait looks back at his vision and his legacy.

## In brief

1. Jean-Claude Jitrois is a French fashion designer, founder of the house that bears his name.
2. He invented stretch leather, a genuine leather made extensible.
3. This innovation made leather a couture material, cut like a noble fabric.
4. His vision rests on the idea of leather as a second skin.
5. Maison Jitrois remains an independent Parisian luxury house built on French savoir-faire.

## The inventor of stretch leather

Jean-Claude Jitrois's major contribution is the invention of [stretch leather]({{< relref lang="en" path="blog/what-is-stretch-leather.md" >}}). Before this innovation, leather remained a structuring material that imposed its shape on the garment. By making it extensible, the designer reversed the relationship: leather now hugs the body instead of constraining it.

This idea, summed up by the image of the **second skin**, opened an entirely new field. Dresses, trousers, jumpsuits and jackets could adopt close-fitting cuts while preserving freedom of movement.

## Leather raised to the rank of couture

Beyond the technique, Jean-Claude Jitrois championed a certain idea of leather: that of a **couture** material in its own right. Working leather with the standards of haute couture requires a specific savoir-faire, detailed in the article on [leather couture]({{< relref lang="en" path="blog/leather-couture-craftsmanship.md" >}}).

This standard is found in every piece, where the quality of the hide, the precision of the cut and the care of the finishing come first. It is what sets a house piece apart from a simple leather item.

## A living legacy

Jean-Claude Jitrois's legacy lives on in the house's current collections, faithful to French manufacturing and the handmade gesture. Stretch leather continues to inspire fitted pieces, such as [leather trousers]({{< relref lang="en" path="blog/how-to-wear-leather-trousers.md" >}}), now a wardrobe classic.

The Parisian luxury house carries on this vision of a supple, elegant leather worn as close to the body as possible. Its creations can be discovered on [jitrois.com](https://jitrois.com/).
