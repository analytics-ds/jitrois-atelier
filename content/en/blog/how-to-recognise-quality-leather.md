Investing in leather means investing in the real thing. Yet how can you be certain of what you're choosing before you commit? The Jitrois Atelier, a house devoted to stretch leather handcrafted in France, shares the criteria that distinguish a true craft from anonymous production. Here is how to recognise quality leather and make a choice that will age beautifully with you.

## Reading the Grain and Natural Marks

Skin is alive. It carries the history of the animal: its scars, insect bites, veins. Full-grain leather preserves all of these signs. They are not flaws; they are proof of authenticity.

When you examine a piece, look for grain consistency unmarred by excessive sanding. A natural, gently irregular grain tells you the original hide has been respected. By contrast, a surface too uniform and glossy suggests corrected-grain leather, where the skin's surface has been sanded, dyed, and sealed. This isn't inferior, but it is less noble than full-grain.

Light traces from the animal's life are entirely normal. What should alert you: deep tears, cracking, or poorly finished gouges. Careful hide selection is already a mark of quality.

## Testing Softness and Elasticity by Touch

Touch never lies. Quality leather announces itself instantly under your fingers.

Run your hand across the surface. It should feel supple, warm, and faintly yielding to pressure, a sign the leather breathes. A good hide is dense without rigidity. It wraps around your hand as a second skin.

Try this test: gently stretch the material between your fingers. Authentic leather yields, then naturally returns to shape. Synthetic remains inert or deforms without spring. That elasticity comes from natural fibres and tannage method.

Test flexibility, too. A fold should form easily, and the crease should gradually fade. If the leather stays stiff even after handling, you are facing either a thick artificial finish or second-grade material.

## Scent Speaks the Truth

Do not hesitate to smell the leather. Scent is a reliable signal that manufacturers cannot fully replicate.

Genuine leather releases a softly woody, natural scent, almost animalic in character. It may be more or less pronounced depending on tannage, but it remains distinctive. It is the aroma you will enjoy when you wear your piece.

If the leather smells of plastic or strong chemicals, this signals either low-grade tannage or a synthetic surface. Some sellers mask chemical odour beneath artificial fragrance. Small details like this speak volumes.

At Jitrois, the scent of our creations shifts slightly from one piece to another, according to the leather type and its region of origin. That variability is our commitment to raw material, unmasked.

## Types of Leather and What They Offer

Not all leathers are equal. Understanding types helps you anticipate how your piece will behave.

**Full-grain leather** is supreme. It is the complete, original surface of the hide, with all its character. It is the most durable, develops a magnificent patina, and grows more beautiful over decades. It is what we favour for our heirloom pieces.

**Nappa leather** offers exceptional suppleness and a smooth finish. It is typically chrome-tanned, making it highly workable. Perfect for pieces that embrace the body, such as our leather couture garments.

**Nubuck** and **suede leather** present a velvety surface, exquisitely soft to the touch. They ask a little more attention, but deliver undeniable elegance.

**Thickness** matters too. Thin leather will be fragile. Thick, dense leather will last lifetimes. At Jitrois, we select hides with the thickness right for each purpose.

## The Atelier's View on Leather Excellence

At Jitrois, rigour extends beyond standard criteria. We do not buy leather from catalogues. We select it hide by hide, directly from tanneries we have trusted for years.

We look first at grain: regular yet alive, telling a story. Next, durability. A hide robust enough to withstand the body's movement, repeated flexing, friction. Then comes the patina-to-come. How will this leather evolve? Will it deepen in character or fade to nothing?

We often choose vegetable or mixed tannage, because it offers better breathability and a more noble patina. But we will not exclude other methods if they serve the vision of a creation.

Finally, we inspect the edge finishing, seaming, assembly. A quality piece is a coherent whole where every detail reinforces the others. What distinguishes a Jitrois creation from a thousand others is precisely this quiet rigour: you feel the quality, even if you cannot always name it.
